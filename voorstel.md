# Universal Carbon Intelligence Platform — Voorstel

## Core Concept

A universal carbon intelligence platform that:

- Aggregates data from all major carbon registries (waar alle data verzameld word en transparant open is)
- Extracts information from:
  - Project Design Documents
  - Monitoring Reports
  - Verification Reports
  - Issuance records
  - Retirement records
  - GIS layers
  - Satellite data
  - Public sales records
- Detects:
  - Double counting risks
  - Land overlap risks
  - Inconsistent issuance patterns
  - Dormant or abandoned projects
  - Delayed monitoring cycles
  - Unrealistic sequestration claims
  - Credit inventory anomalies
- Generates:
  - Integrity scores
  - Risk ratings
  - Liquidity signals
  - Market alerts
  - Automated due diligence summaries

The platform does not replace standards (the registries).
It becomes the intelligence infrastructure above standards.

## The Real Market Gap

Carbon data exists — but nobody truly connects it.

Today:
- Verra sees Verra
- Gold Standard sees Gold Standard
- Buyers see PDFs
- Brokers see listings
- Governments see fragmented reports

Nobody sees:
- the entire land history,
- cross-registry risks,
- timing inconsistencies,
- inactive inventories,
- suspicious issuance behavior,
- or real liquidity.

**Positioning: "The Bloomberg Terminal of carbon markets."**

The carbon market already has enough registries.
What it lacks is: unified intelligence, automated integrity monitoring, and transparent liquidity visibility.

## Architecture

### Layer 1 — Registry Ingestion Engine

Continuously scrapes or ingests:
- Registry databases
- Public documents
- GIS coordinates
- Satellite imagery
- Issuance histories
- Retirement logs
- Ownership transfers

**Output:** Structured machine-readable carbon data.

---

### Layer 2 — AI Interpretation Engine

The AI reads:
- PDDs
- Monitoring reports
- Validation comments
- Methodologies
- Leakage calculations
- Baseline assumptions
- Permanence risks

Then converts them into:
- Standardized datasets
- Project narratives
- Risk flags
- Comparable metrics

**Example:** A 300-page ARR monitoring report becomes:

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Project area | 14,200 ha |
| Species mix | Agroforestry teak/mango |
| Leakage risk | Medium |
| Survival estimate confidence | Low |
| Soil carbon measured? | No |
| NDVI trend | Positive |
| Issuance velocity | Unusual |
| Buffer adequacy | Weak |

---

### Layer 3 — Integrity Detection System

#### A. Overlap Detection

The system checks:
- Parcel overlaps
- Polygon similarities
- Nearby duplicated claims
- Repeated coordinates
- Conflicting land ownership
- Jurisdictional overlap

#### B. Issuance Intelligence

Example logic:
> "Project issued 400,000 credits. Only 40,000 retired. No active sales activity for 14 months. Monitoring overdue by 9 months."

Result: automated flag, risk adjustment, market alert, temporary "watch status."

#### C. Dynamic Risk Scoring

Every project gets:
- Transparency score
- Permanence score
- Delivery confidence
- Monitoring reliability
- Financial activity score
- Registry consistency score

Almost like a credit rating agency.

---

### Layer 4 — Market Layer

Instead of locking projects into one ecosystem, projects can:
- Connect their registry accounts
- Expose verified inventory
- List available credits dynamically
- Receive automated liquidity discovery

**Important distinction:** Not creating "new credits." Creating a universal visibility and trading intelligence layer.
